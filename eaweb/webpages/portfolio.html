Internship with Enigma Solutions at ETI

In the late spring and summer of 2020 I was an intern at Emerging Technology Institute in Red Springs, NC under Enigma Solutions military contractor. Enigma Solutions was lead by Aaron Carr and was looking for interns to be a part of the team to realize the concept of a project for SOCOM. This project was named "BISKIT" which stood for "Basic Internet Starting Kit". During my time in community college, I was looking to get some work experience under my belt so that I can get a full-time position working in tech. This opportunity revealed itself and I took it. I was hired onto the team to work on the BISKIT project.

The BISKIT Project

BISKIT was a large kit that housed a number of sensors and gadgets that would connect to a Raspberry Pi 4. Accomanying the kit was a learning management system style website that teaches users (US SOCOM soldiers) different useful applications that could be made to aid in their opporations as well as including a Python course.

Before working as an intern at Enigma Solutions, I did not have all the skills associated to build the LMS and teach the courses, however, I learned as I went and picked up many new skills. One of the most useful skills I picked up was learning the basics of Python. I went on to use those skills later to dabble in personal projects as well as ace a needed Python course during my undergrad Computer Science major. I also learned how to organize projects and complete them. BISKIT was a very messy experience at first, but I learned how to manage my tasks quickly and contribute to the team. Lastly, I learned how to showcase projects to expos during my time with Enigma Solutions. This internship gave me a lot of experience with software engineering and product development.
